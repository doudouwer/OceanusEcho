# OceanusEcho — Presentation Script (English)
*Target length: ~3 minutes*

---

## ACT I — Profile Sailor Shift

### Scene 1: The Inflection Point

**Interaction:**
Click **ACT 1** in the left navigation.
The right panel auto-focuses on **Career Arc**.
Look for the peak around 2028 — the yellow Notable nodes are the telltale signal.

**Voiceover:**

> "We begin with **Sailor Shift**, the artist at the heart of OceanusEcho. The timeline shows steady output through the early 2020s. Then 2028 arrives — song count peaks and **Notable tags surge in lockstep**. That is the moment she crossed the threshold from prolific creator to cultural force."

---

### Scene 2: The Musical Lineage

**Interaction:**
Click the bottom **Next** button to switch to **Influence Galaxy**.
Toggle **IN_STYLE_OF** in the relation filter bar at the top.
Observe the inbound edges radiating from Sailor Shift.

**Voiceover:**

> "No artist emerges from a vacuum. Switch to the Influence Galaxy and filter by **InStyleOf** — this decodes her musical DNA. These edges trace back to the originators of Oceanus Folk. Sailor Shift inherited, refined, and amplified that sound into a modern pop context."

---

### Scene 3: The Ivy Echoes Legacy

**Interaction:**
In the relation filter bar, toggle **MEMBER_OF** and **PERFORMER_OF** simultaneously.
Look for the **Ivy Echoes** cluster in the network.

**Voiceover:**

> "The most compelling chapter: her former band, **Ivy Echoes**. Filter by MemberOf and PerformerOf and a small circle expands into a massive influence matrix. After the breakup, each member became a new hub in the network — this cascade is the clearest proof of Sailor Shift's role as a **bridge node**."

---

## ACT II — Map the Spread of Oceanus Folk

### Scene 1: Spike or Slow Burn?

**Interaction:**
Click **ACT 2** in the left navigation.
The single **Genre Flow** panel loads Scene 1 automatically.
Look for the steep spike around **2022**.

**Voiceover:**

> "Now we shift scale — from a single artist to an entire genre. **Oceanus Folk** did not explode overnight. Before 2022, it sat at a low, steady plateau. Then 2022 hit — one sharp vertical spike. The year-over-year delta in that single year exceeds 45% of total growth. This is the breakout signal we came looking for."

---

### Scene 2: Cross-Genre Convergence

**Interaction:**
Click the bottom **Next** button to advance to Scene 2 — the **Genre Carousel**.
Watch the comparison genre cycle automatically.

**Voiceover:**

> "Genres are never isolated islands. The carousel cycles through every genre sharing space with Oceanus Folk. The dashed fusion window locks onto the **year of maximum overlap** — the moment two genres peak together. By the time the spike in Scene 1 registers, this fusion has already been building for years."

---

### Scene 3: Where Does It Flow?

**Interaction:**
Click the bottom **Next** button to advance to Scene 3 — the **Style-Edge Sankey**.
Focus on the widest flow paths — the genres receiving the most influence from Oceanus Folk.

**Voiceover:**

> "To quantify influence, we use a **Sankey diagram** built from IN_STYLE_OF edge aggregation. Path width represents how much Oceanus Folk's output flows into neighboring genres. Oceanus Folk has become a **core aesthetic source**, transmitting its signature sound through these data fibers into the broader music landscape. The breakout was not a solo broadcast — it was a relay."

---

## ACT III — Predict Rising Stars

### Scene 1: The Eve of Explosion

**Interaction:**
Click **ACT 3** in the left navigation.
The **Career Arc** panel loads first — green candidate lines overlay Sailor Shift's blue trajectory.
Watch the carousel cycle through candidates.

**Voiceover:**

> "Now the question: **who is next?** We overlay each candidate's annual output on Sailor Shift's pre-breakout timeline. We hunt for a shape: **a long accumulation phase followed by a synchronized surge in song count and Notable tags**. That simultaneous lift in volume and quality is the signature of an artist about to make the same leap Sailor Shift made in 2028."

---

### Scene 2: Social Gravity in the Network

**Interaction:**
The bottom **Influence Galaxy** panel shows each candidate's network position.
Bridge nodes and degree centrality are highlighted as candidates cycle.

**Voiceover:**

> "But trajectory alone is not enough. A high-growth artist on the periphery of the graph will not become a genre anchor. We look for **bridge nodes** — candidates whose collaborations span multiple genres. The combination of a steep career slope **and** high cross-genre connectivity separates a genuine breakout candidate from a one-season wonder."

---

### Scene 3: The Final Spotlight

**Interaction:**
The **Star Profiler** radar panel auto-settles.
Sailor Shift's polygon anchors the chart; the top candidate's polygon overlays it.
The ranking list stops cycling — the **top-ranked candidate** is revealed.

**Voiceover:**

> "Finally, the **Star Profiler** — a radar chart distilling four key dimensions: output momentum, cross-genre reach, network centrality, and genre entropy. Each candidate is scored on all four. The candidate whose polygon **most closely overlaps with Sailor Shift's** is the most data-supported prediction. The data speaks clearly."

---

## ACT IV — Explore the Ecosystem

**Interaction:**
Click **"Explore the Ecosystem"** in the top-right corner of the page.
The browser opens the full interactive dashboard.

**Voiceover:**

> "**Explore the Ecosystem** opens the full interactive dashboard — search any artist, filter by year and genre, and watch charts respond in real time. Panels are **live-linked**: brushing a year range instantly refocuses the network graph. You can compare up to three artists, toggle relationship types, or switch to a Sankey view. The ecosystem is yours to explore."
